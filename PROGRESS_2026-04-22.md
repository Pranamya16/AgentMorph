## What is AgentMorph, in one paragraph?

AgentMorph is a system that **tests AI agents** — the kind that use large language models (ChatGPT-style) to pick and call tools on your behalf, like "search the store, add the kettle to my cart, check out." The problem with these agents is there's usually no right answer to compare against. So how do you know when they're broken?

Our trick: **change the question in a way that shouldn't matter, and see if the agent changes its answer.** If you shuffle the order of tools in the agent's menu, or reword "find me a kettle" as "locate a kettle for me," a reliable agent should behave the same. If it doesn't, we've caught a bug. This is the same idea as metamorphic testing for software — you can't always check the answer directly, but you can check that equivalent inputs give equivalent answers. Nobody has done this at scale for tool-using agents before, which is why we're aiming at the NeurIPS Datasets & Benchmarks venue.

## The two numbers that matter

**1,000 mutation pairs** — the size of the experiment. Five language models times ten rules times twenty scenarios. Why these numbers? Five models covers the open-source landscape without overloading the GPU budget. Ten rules covers the main categories of invariance a reliable agent should respect. Twenty scenarios per rule is enough to be statistically meaningful (the paper will show confidence intervals) without inflating the dataset beyond what fits in a Colab session.

**~150 bugs** — the expected size of the HuggingFace dataset. Prior work on similar robustness tests finds 10–20% divergence rates on open-weight models. At 15% we'd get 150 bugs out of 1,000 pairs. Enough for per-model, per-rule breakdowns in the paper and still a real public resource.

## Risks still open

Three realistic things that could go wrong between now and May 4:

1. **The overnight sweep could crash partway through.** Colab sessions die unpredictably. Our mitigation: the runner is fully resumable. A crash at hour 12 of 17 costs us at most one scenario — we re-launch and keep going from where we stopped.

2. **Some of the cached paraphrases could be low quality.** Gemini occasionally produces near-identical or degenerate rephrases. Our mitigation: if a rule hits a bad paraphrase during the sweep, it falls back to a template rewrite and logs a "low-confidence" flag, so the paper can exclude those rows from the headline numbers if needed.

3. **Human validation on the bug sample might slip.** The plan calls for 2–3 lab-mates to validate 50 bugs by May 3. If they don't deliver in time, Plan B is to skip human validation for the initial submission and note it as "in-progress" in the paper — it's a nice-to-have, not a gating deliverable.
